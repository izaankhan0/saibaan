One Tree at a Time

THE SAYEBAAN PROJECT

About Us

The Sayebaan Project works with schools and industries to mobilise their staff and students to take ownership over their natural environment by planting trees and caring for them. We provide a tailored programme for each institution that we work with, providing them with ecological planning, procurement of appropriate and cost effective plants, and liaison with municipal authorities to secure permissions for plantation. Moreover, we provide an online map based database to log all trees planted and check their progress using periodic pictures.

OUR PROJECTS

Karachi Grammar School

The Directors of the Sayebaan Project founded the Lignum Afforestation Project at the Karachi Grammar School which is a vibrant community formed around afforestation. After planting several dozen trees in the school campus students adopted Family Park, a KMC park near the campus and made a small specialised urban forest. We enabled students to lead every stage of the process from procurement to plantation and maintenance.

Prestige Kitchens Pvt Ltd

The Sayebaan Project partnered with Prestige Kitchens, a furniture manufacturer company to plant trees near their industrial facilities in Port Qasim. Sayebaan enabled them to conduct a staff-led plantation and trained them to take ownership over their area and maintain their plant life. Sayebaan also provided them with environmental consultation enabling them to secure an ISO 140001 certification.

ADA Magazine

Sayebaan Collaborated with ADA Magazine by distributing Neem and Gulmohar saplings at their award ceremony held in Shalimar Gardens Lahore, these were designed to align with ADA’s desire for greater social consciousness and a positive environmental impact. ADA (Architecture, Design, Art) is Pakistan’s premier Art and Design publication and its biannually awarded prizes are highly coveted locally and internationally.

L2L Academy

We held a plantation and workshop with students at L2L Academy school in Bahadurabad. We planted Moringa, Neem and Badaam trees in an abandoned ground adjacent to the school and taught students to care for these plants. Plants were logged onto our digital database.
